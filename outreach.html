Pick the target

Outcome: a locked campaign strategy. Niche, ICP, competitor whitespace, and the method, all decided before a single email goes out.

Outreach is the second mouth of attract. Content pulls people in. Outbound goes and gets them. But volume aimed at the wrong people is just faster failure, so the work starts before the tools. You pick the target first.

Four questions answered, in order. Which niche. Who exactly inside it, the named role that signs. Where your competitors are not, the whitespace you can own. And the method, whether you lead with a direct offer, an audit, an interview, a masterclass. Get these wrong and no subject line saves you.

This is the one lesson where you slow down. Run the strategy skill, let it interview you and pull the competitor reads, and do not touch a lead list until the four questions have hard answers.

Do this

  1. Run the campaign-strategy skill on your offer and let it pressure-test the niche.
  2. Name the exact decision-maker. The title, not the company.
  3. Find the whitespace and lock the method before you build anything.
Download · the campaign-strategy skill
Done when: you can say your niche, your buyer, your angle, and your method in one breath.

Key takeaway: aim before you fire. Volume only pays off when it is pointed at the right person with the right angle.

Build the lead list

Outcome: a clean list of named decision-makers, segmented by fit, each one carrying a researched angle.

A list is not a pile of emails. It is named people, sorted by how they buy, each with a reason you are reaching out to them and not someone else. The AI does the gathering and the research. You set the rules.

Here is what good looks like. A 250-lead list of named RIA decision-makers, zero wirehouse, split into four firm-size segments, each segment with its own angle. That is not a scrape. That is a list that already knows what to say to each row.

The builder pulls the names, drops the ones who do not fit, runs research on each, and writes the per-segment angle. You skim it and cut anyone who feels off. Cleaner list beats bigger list every time.

Do this

  1. Hand the builder your locked ICP and the segments you want.
  2. Let it pull, dedupe, research, and tag each lead with its angle.
  3. Skim the result and cut the rows that do not belong.
Download · the lead-list builder
Done when: every row is a named human, in the right segment, with a reason you are writing them.

Key takeaway: the list is the campaign. Segmented and researched beats long and cold.

Cold email that lands

Outcome: a full email sequence written on a data-backed playbook, in your voice, ready to load.

The list says who. Now you write what. Not one email, a sequence: the opener that earns the open, the follow-ups that do most of the work, and the breakup that closes the loop. Each one short, each one a single ask.

You are not guessing at this. The scripter runs on a playbook built from millions of sends and tens of thousands of real positive replies, so the openers, the cadence, and the breakup are patterns that already work. Then it writes them in your voice from the soul file, so volume never reads like a template.

Prompt: Write a four-step cold email sequence for this segment. Short, one clear ask per email. Opener, two follow-ups, a breakup. Use my voice file. No fluff, no AI tells.

Do this

  1. Run the cold-email-scripter against one segment and its angle.
  2. Read it out loud. If a line does not sound like you, cut it.
  3. Lock the sequence per segment, ready for the sender.
Download · the cold-email-scripter skill
Done when: you have a tight sequence per segment that sounds like you wrote it on a good day.

Key takeaway: the follow-ups do the work. Write the whole sequence, in your voice, on a playbook that already won.

LinkedIn and DMs

Outcome: a warm outbound lane running alongside the cold one, profile to post to DM, on a proven growth playbook.

Cold email is one door. LinkedIn is the warmer one. The buyer sees your profile, your posts, and your message, all at once, so trust is built before the DM ever lands. Two lanes hitting the same list doubles the ways in.

This is not random connecting. The engine runs a full play: a profile that converts the visit, posts that warm the room, then DMs that open a real conversation. Same buyer from the email list, reached a second way, on a playbook that has scaled accounts to millions of followers.

Do this

  1. Run the linkedin-growth-engine to tighten the profile first. That is the page everyone lands on.
  2. Set the post cadence that warms your buyer before you reach out.
  3. Open DMs with the named leads from your list, not a cold pitch.
Download · the linkedin-growth-engine skill
Done when: your profile converts, your posts warm the room, and your DMs start conversations instead of pitches.

Key takeaway: warm outbound is just outbound to someone who already saw you. Run it next to the cold lane, not instead of it.

Sequences and sending

Outcome: the campaign loaded into the sender, personalized per lead, scheduled, waiting on your go.

You have the list and the copy. Now the machine carries the volume. Load it into the sender, Instantly or PlusVibe, map the variables per lead so each email fills in the right name, firm, and angle, and set the schedule. The system sends. You do not sit and click.

Send on the days that get answered. Tuesday to Friday, business hours for the buyer. The sender drips it out at a human pace across your inboxes instead of blasting it in one suspicious burst. Nothing fires until you say go.

Do this

  1. Upload the segment and its sequence into Instantly or PlusVibe.
  2. Map the per-lead variables so every email reads personal.
  3. Schedule Tuesday to Friday, then flip it on when you are ready.

Done when: the campaign is loaded, personalized, scheduled, and one click from live.

Deliverability

Outcome: your emails actually reach the inbox, not the spam folder. The whole machine is worthless if the mail does not land.

You can have the best list and the best copy and still get nothing, because the mail never arrived. Deliverability is the unglamorous layer that decides whether all the work upstream counts. You handle it before you scale, not after the replies dry up.

  • Warmup. New inboxes build reputation slowly, sending and receiving real-looking mail for weeks before you push volume. Instantly runs this for you. Do not skip the ramp.
  • Spam-checking. Run the copy through a spam-word and formatting check before it ships, so a single phrase does not tank the whole sequence.
  • Inbox placement. Test where you actually land, the primary inbox versus spam, and watch it as you scale. The number that matters is how many landed, not how many sent.

Do this

  1. Confirm warmup has run on every sending inbox before you go live.
  2. Spam-check the sequence copy and fix anything that flags.
  3. Run an inbox-placement test and only scale once you are landing.
Download · the deliverability checklist
Done when: a placement test shows your mail landing in the inbox, and you only then turn up the volume.

Key takeaway: deliverability is the floor the whole machine stands on. Land first, scale second.
